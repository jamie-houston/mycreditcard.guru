bolt Quick Start Guide

Get up and running with Credit Card Guru in three easy steps.

looks_one 1. Set Up Your Profile & Cards

Go to the My Cards page to add the credit cards you currently hold. For the most accurate calculations, click Edit Details on your added cards to specify nicknames, cardholders (if multiple household members), opening dates, and signup bonus history.

looks_two 2. Enter Your Spending

On the Roadmap page, fill in the monthly spending grid to reflect your average expenditures across key categories like Dining, Groceries, Gas, and Travel. The recommendation engine uses this exact budget to calculate the ongoing cash back or rewards values of your portfolios.

looks_3 3. Optimize Your Portfolio

Select your preferred strategy (e.g. Cashback vs Points, and Effort/Complexity levels) to immediately generate your 12-Month Recommendation Plan. You will get a step-by-step roadmap indicating which cards to keep, cancel, or apply for next, along with the optimized card choice to swipe for each spending category.

route Roadmap Choices & Actions

Understanding the recommendations generated in your roadmap.

check_circle Keep Recommendations

Keep denotes cards in your current wallet that should remain active. This occurs because the ongoing category reward earnings or the annualized value of the card's recurring benefits (like statement credits) outweigh any annual fee.

cancel Cancel Recommendations

Cancel indicates cards that have an annual fee but do not earn enough rewards or offer enough recurring credits to justify keeping. Canceling them net-optimizes your annualized portfolio value.

add_circle Apply Recommendations

Apply shows new cards you should obtain. This recommendation is based on a high signup bonus value or long-term reward rates that fit your spending profile, scheduled within issuer-specific application rules.

settings Strategy Options & Controls

Tailor recommendations to your reward goals and effort preferences.

payments Cashback vs. Points & Miles

You can choose your primary optimization goal:

  • Cashback Strategy: Values point currencies at a flat 1.0¢/point statement credit rate. Ideal for simple, cash-equivalent savings.
  • Points & Miles (Travel) Strategy: Leverages boosted valuations for loyalty programs (e.g. valuing Chase Ultimate Rewards at 1.5¢/point, and Amex Membership Rewards at 1.3¢/point) assuming optimization via travel partners.
construction Effort-Tolerance Presets

Effort presets restrict the maximum number of new card applications and active cards in your final portfolio:

  • Low Effort: Limits your portfolio to a maximum of 3 cards and permits only 1 new card application. Best for keeping your wallet simple.
  • Medium Effort (Recommended): Restricts your portfolio to a maximum of 5 cards and permits up to 2 new card applications. Balance between complexity and rewards.
  • Max Value: Unleashes the algorithm to recommend up to 4 new card applications with no overall card count limits, squeezing out every possible dollar.
security Issuer Spacing Rules Toggle

By default, the engine models strict issuer rules (like Chase 5/24, Bank of America 2/3/4, or Capital One 1/6-month velocity rules). In the Advanced panel, you can toggle the "Issuer Spacing Rules" checkbox to ignore velocity checks for theoretical analysis.

style Card Details & Custom Settings

Customize individual card parameters to align with your personal credit history.

badge Card Nicknames

Giving cards nicknames (e.g., "Jamie's Gold Card" vs. "Taylor's Gold Card") helps you distinguish cards when managing them in a multi-player household. These nicknames are displayed throughout your customized category optimization layout and roadmap recommendations.

calendar_month Opening & Closing Dates

Specifying the exact opening date of your cards is crucial for the recommendation engine because issuer velocity and approval rules rely on age-based checks. For example:

  • Chase 5/24: Checks the count of cards you've opened across all issuers within the last 24 months.
  • BofA 2/3/4: Checks card approvals within 2, 12, and 24 months.
  • Velocity limits: Spacing between applications from the same issuer (e.g., Capital One limits applications to 1 per 6 months).

If you close a card, setting a Closing Date in the interface soft-closes the card. It preserves the historical opening date for velocity checks while marking the card as inactive for future category rewards optimization.

celebration Signup Bonus Custom Override

By default, the engine infers whether you are eligible to receive a new card's signup bonus based on issuer-specific rules (like Chase Sapphire's 48-month family rule or Amex's once-per-lifetime constraint). You can override this auto-inference under Edit Details:

  • Auto: Inferred automatically based on rules and your card opening/closed history.
  • Earned/Ineligible: Force the engine to treat you as ineligible for the bonus, valuing it at $0. Useful if you recently earned it or received it through non-standard channels.
  • Not Earned/Eligible: Force the engine to count the signup bonus, bypassing automated rule blocks.

calculate How the Recommendation Engine Works

Under the hood of Credit Card Guru's mathematical optimization.

mediation Greedy Search Optimization

To identify the best combination of cards, the engine evaluates thousands of combinations using a multi-step search:

  1. Ongoing Category Rewards: The engine matches your monthly spending budget against each card's reward multipliers, assigning spending to the highest earning card per category.
  2. Net Annualized Value: It calculates the total value of each portfolio option.
    Formula
    Portfolio Value = Category Rewards + Counted Signup Bonuses + Credits Value - Annual Fees
  3. Greedy Selection: Starting with your currently held cards, the engine iteratively tests candidate portfolios, seeking the highest net value that satisfies your selected Effort-Tolerance constraints.
account_balance_wallet Points Program Pooling

Many credit card programs allow you to pool points or transfer them to premium cards to unlock higher redemption rates. The engine automatically models this. For example:

  • If you hold the Chase Sapphire Reserve (valuing Chase UR points at 1.5¢ each), any points earned on a Chase Freedom Flex or Chase Freedom Unlimited (normally valued at 1.0¢ each) are automatically boosted and valued at the higher 1.5¢ rate.
  • The same rule applies to Amex Membership Rewards pooling.

When this occurs, a helper badge "Points valued via [Redemption Card]" will appear under the card's line item rewards breakdown to keep the calculations completely transparent.

groups Multi-Player Households

Credit Card Guru supports player entities in a single household (e.g. Primary Player, Player 2, and Business Entities). Households pool their spending budget and share the category reward allocation, but the engine models eligibility rules individually:

  • Velocity rules like Chase 5/24 or Amex caps are checked against each individual player's card history.
  • The engine recommends who should apply for a new card. For instance, if Player 1 is at 5/24 but Player 2 is at 2/24, the roadmap will direct Player 2 to apply for the Chase card.
  • Business cards are automatically routed to business entities (falling back to sole proprietorship under the primary player if no business entity is declared).
  • Card ownership follows the same rule: a business card you add is assigned to your business entity, a personal card to your personal primary. If your household has more than one player, or more than one business, you'll be asked which one when you add or edit a card — otherwise it's assigned automatically with no prompt.
hourglass_empty 12-Month Signup Bonus Capacity

It is unrealistic to apply for many credit cards simultaneously, as you might not spend enough to meet all minimum spending requirements. The engine schedules new card applications across a 12-month budget:

  • Each recommended card's minimum spending requirement consumes a chunk of your monthly spend over its required window (e.g., spending $4,000 in 3 months consumes $1,333/month of budget).
  • The engine walks the timeline and caps applications once your monthly spending budget is fully consumed.
  • Over-budget bonuses are labeled as Deferred. They are shown in your roadmap with a $0 signup value for the current year (but can still be recommended on the strength of their long-term category reward rates alone).
shopping_cart Upcoming Expense Mode

If you have a large one-off purchase planned (e.g., home remodeling, wedding expenses, taxes), you can enter the amount and category in the Upcoming large purchase section of the Roadmap builder. The engine runs a dedicated analysis:

  • It calculates which of your existing cards is the best to swipe based on category rates.
  • It ranks new cards to apply for, assessing whether the large expense helps you reach high minimum spending thresholds that would normally be out of reach with your regular monthly spend.
  • It combines the signup bonus, category rewards, and annual fee into a simple, three-term sum to show your net purchase yield.
